Privacy Policy

Your privacy is at the heart of everything we do. This policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you visit Sprouting Nature World.

📅 Last updated: 17 June 2026

Welcome to Sprouting Nature World. We are committed to protecting your personal data and respecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy outlines how we handle your information when you use our website, services, and communications.

By using our site, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you have any questions, please contact us.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect and process the following types of personal data:

  • Identity Data: Name, username, or similar identifier.
  • Contact Data: Email address, postal address, phone number.
  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, device information, and cookies (see our Cookies Policy).
  • Usage Data: Information about how you use our website, pages visited, and interactions.
  • Communications: Any correspondence you send to us via forms, email, or social media.

We do not collect sensitive personal data (e.g., racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, health information) unless you voluntarily provide it.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your data for the following legitimate purposes:

  • To provide, maintain, and improve our website and content.
  • To respond to your inquiries, comments, or requests (e.g., via our contact form).
  • To send you updates, newsletters, or promotional materials (only if you have opted in).
  • To analyze website usage and enhance user experience.
  • To comply with legal obligations and protect our rights.

We will never sell or rent your personal data to third parties.

3. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the UK GDPR and EU GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Consent: When you have given clear consent for us to process your data (e.g., for marketing emails).
  • Contract: When processing is necessary for a contract you have with us (e.g., if you book a consultation).
  • Legitimate Interests: When it is necessary for our legitimate interests, provided your rights do not override those interests (e.g., improving our services).
  • Legal Obligation: When we need to comply with a legal or regulatory obligation.

4. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes we collected it for, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements.

To determine the appropriate retention period, we consider the amount, nature, and sensitivity of the data, the potential risk of harm from unauthorised use or disclosure, and applicable legal requirements.

5. Data Security

We have implemented appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data from accidental loss, unauthorised access, alteration, or disclosure. These include:

  • Encryption of data in transit (SSL/TLS).
  • Access controls and authentication.
  • Regular security assessments and monitoring.

While we strive to protect your data, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. You share data at your own risk, but we take every precaution to safeguard it.

6. Your Rights

Under data protection law, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to access: You can request a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification: You can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure: You can request deletion of your data under certain circumstances.
  • Right to restrict processing: You can ask us to limit how we use your data.
  • Right to data portability: You can request a copy of your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Right to object: You can object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.
  • Right to withdraw consent: If we rely on your consent, you can withdraw it at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us. We will respond within 30 days.

7. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to external sites (e.g., recommended partners, social media platforms). We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of such third-party sites. We encourage you to read their privacy policies before providing any personal data.

8. Cookies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience, analyse traffic, and personalise content. For detailed information, please see our Cookies Policy.

9. Children’s Privacy

Sprouting Nature World is not intended for children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with data, please contact us, and we will take steps to delete that information.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal obligations. We will notify you of any significant changes by posting the new policy on this page with an updated "Last updated" date.

We encourage you to review this policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your data.
